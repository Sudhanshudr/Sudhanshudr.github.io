About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Electrical Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Jodhpur. My research focuses on developing artificial intelligence methods for cardiovascular disease diagnosis using electrocardiogram (ECG) signals. I am particularly interested in designing deep learning models that are accurate, computationally efficient, and suitable for deployment in real-world healthcare applications. My research interests include ECG foundation models, lightweight deep learning architectures, model compression and quantization, multimodal learning, explainable AI, adversarial robustness, and data-efficient learning. A significant part of my work focuses on developing AI models that can run efficiently on resource-constrained edge devices while maintaining high diagnostic performance. My research has contributed to areas such as arrhythmia classification, ECG-based cardiac function assessment, efficient ECG foundation models, and robust deep learning techniques for biomedical signal analysis. My work has been presented at leading international conferences, including IEEE EMBC and IEEE ISCAS, and published in high-impact biomedical engineering journals. I am passionate about translating advances in artificial intelligence into practical healthcare solutions that are reliable, efficient, and accessible.
